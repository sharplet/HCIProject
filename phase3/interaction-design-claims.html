Claims for interaction scenarios

The index will be accessible via the drop-down menu.(Interaction Scenario 1)
 + Easily accessible at all times with two taps of the finger.
- But this button could take up valuable space within the drop-down menu.

The hyperlinks associated with the concept map will be displayed after a tap of the finger within the right margin of any given page. (Interaction Scenario 1)
 + Links will not clutter up the page at all times.
+ Links will be easily accessible at all times.
- but the user could accidentally bring them up at undesired times resulting in frustration.
- the user could forget how to bring up the links, or not know how to do it in the first place.

Navigating the textbook will be done primarily with the zoom out feature. (Interaction Scenario 2)
 + Easy to use
+ Intuitive
+ Fun to use
- but could potentially be difficult to scan pages for content due to zoomed out view.

Pop-up menus will be used when interacting with the text. (Interaction Scenario 2)
 + Reduces clutter.
- but could get rather annoying when accidentally initiated by the user.

Highlighting will be done by swiping over text. (Interaction Scenario 2)
 + Provides direct manipulation.
- Users could potentially highlight the unintended text.
