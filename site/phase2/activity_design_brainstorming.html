Discussion with client about activity design

Initially, the group and the client had differing points of view when it came the purpose of a digital textbook application. The group, having been given the broad task of designing a digital textbook, approached the task from the point of view of improving on existing eBook technologies. However, the client introduced the idea of concept mapping as a way to relate concepts, and showed an example concept map implemented using hypertext in a web browser. The group decided to combine both points of view to design a textbook platform for iPad with the dual focuses of providing better usability than currently available eBook readers, and also using concept mapping techniques to enhance the learning experience for users.

From this discussion and the idea of links to related concepts at the paragraph, section and chapter levels, the client suggested that if a user is completely lost regarding a given textbook, they can follow the concept map up the hierarchy until they reach a foundational concept that they are familiar with. From that point, they can read the chain of related concepts, building their knowledge until they understand the text they were originally reading.

This functionality is currently in our list of activities, but will be added at a later date. (This is due to the group not meeting with the client until towards the end of phase 2, as suggested in the phase description.)
